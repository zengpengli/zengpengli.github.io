Hi, I am currently an associate professor with the School of Cyber Science and Technology at Shandong University (SDU) Qingdao Campus and supported by the SDU Young Scholars Future Program. Prior to joining SDU, I was a postdoctoral research fellow at the Singapore University of Technology & Design (SUTD). I worked with Dr. Paweł Szałachowski and Prof. Jianying Zhou on post-quantum distributed computing, e.g., blockchain consensus protocol. Prior to joining SUTD, I was a postdoctoral research associate at Lancaster University from Jan. 2018, working with Dr. Bingsheng Zhang and Dr. Jan Willemson on cryptographic e-voting and zero-knowledge proof, etc. My postdoctoral works with my advisors are one part of Cybernetica and IOHK.

I completed my doctoral oral defense in December 2017 under the supervision of Prof. Chunguang Ma, and have benefited from the long-term academic mentorship of Prof. Ding Wang. During my doctoral program, I have worked with Prof. Steven Galbraith and Prof. Hong-Sheng Zhou, respectively, as a research assistant (and a joint Ph.D. program student) from Nov. 2015 - Oct. 2017.

Research Interests: My primary research interests are real-world cryptographic protocols and privacy-enhancing technologies for real-world applications. Recently, specific topics include: threshold multiparty computing, private communication, secure messaging, and password-based cryptography.

Please don't hesitate to email me if you are interested in my research. I'm looking for passionate and motivated students to drive my projects. (Basically, I could provide 0-2 doctoral student positions and 3-4 master's student positions from 2022.)
